Welcome to the Computer Science program! We hope you enjoy these next four years with a stellar laptop at your side. Trust us, having the right laptop will make a world of difference. We have developed a set of criteria to help you choose the laptop that is right for you and ranked seven popular models as examples.

We realize we can't rank every laptop out there so we picked the best selling laptops listed on Amazon, excluding tablets, chromebooks, and non-Intel CPUs. We chose to gather all our data, including laptop specifications, from Amazon because it is the largest online laptop retailer. It is also unaffiliated with specific laptop brands. How we did it.

In order to calculate the score for each laptop, we first gave each criterion a certain weight, so that all the weights added up to 100. We then developed a way to calculate a score for each criterion based on the specifications of the laptop, which we then scaled to the correct weight. We also determined the highest reasonable limit for each criterion in order to scale correctly. To do this, we made some assumptions about the highest reasonable limit for each of them. What we found.

When we ran our algorithm on the data, we discovered that according to our scale the HP Envy Quad Edition was the best laptop for a Computer Science Major. Its price is mid-range at just over $700, but it has a quad core 2.4 GHz processor, a 15.6” screen, 8 Gb of RAM, and a terabyte of hard drive space. While it is relatively heavy and doesn’t have a solid-state hard drive, those are not enough to counteract the decent price and great power. The Dell and Toshiba came in second and third, respectively, due to their incredibly low price rather in spite of their subpar tech specs. The MacBook Pro had the highest user rating as well as fairly light and reasonably powerful, but the high price tag was insurmountable. The ASUS was a brick and moderately expensive. However it was very powerful, so a label of portable desktop is very accurate. The MacBook Air was incredibly light and moderately priced, however in important categories of screen size, power, and storage it was simply outclassed by the opposition.

What we thought.

We were satisfied with the results of our calculation. It successfully ranked the laptops according to their specifications and our criteria. However, the algorithm is objective, and purchasing a laptop is highly subjective. Most people will buy a laptop according to their personal preferences, regardless of the subjective data. For example, our personal favorite is the MacBook Pro. We would be willing to spend the extra money for it because we prefer OSX to Windows. Objectively, we can see why the HP did so well as it is high-powered with a moderate price tag. We hope that our article helps you as you choose the special companion that will support you in these coming years. But at least if you make a poor choice this companion is not eternal as you can always buy a new one.
